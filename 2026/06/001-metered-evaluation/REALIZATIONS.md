### How we reached it — the naive question, the half-derivation, the catch, the synthesis

It opened in honest not-knowing — the same register as the *"is bytecode like shellcode?"* question before it: *"what actually is a JIT?… is this just a dumbass name for memoization?… or like an LRU?"* The apparatus answered plainly — a compiler that runs at runtime, lazily, on the hot parts; **not** memoization (that caches the *answer* keyed by input; a JIT caches a faster *version of the code*), **not** an LRU (that is an eviction policy a JIT might *contain*, never the JIT itself).
